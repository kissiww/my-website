During this time I completed two big sections on freeCodeCamp: Design and Absolute & Relative Units.


Design

Design taught me things I was already doing without knowing their names. Turns out there's actual vocabulary for what I was just doing by instinct:

  • Composition — arranging elements so they relate to each other harmoniously
  • Visual hierarchy — guiding the user's eye through information in the right order
  • White space — the empty area around elements that makes everything breathe
  • Balance — distributing visual weight to create a sense of equilibrium
  • Contrast — not just black on white, but making things work across all screens and lighting
  • Alignment — making a layout look organized and visually clean

Absolute & Relative Units

Units were a bit of a headache at first. But now I actually understand why my video-container was once breaking the entire layout (yes, that was a width: 2000px situation. we don't talk about it).


Here's what I learned:

  • px
  • em
  • rem
  • %
  • vw
  • vh
  • calc()
  • min-height
  • max-width

The most useful ones for me right now are vw and vh — they scale relative to the viewport, which is exactly what you need for responsive layouts. And calc() feels like actual magic — for example min-height: calc(100vh - 100px) to account for padding.


My anime project

The biggest thing I did during this time was start my own project — an anime page that I added to this very website.


Here's what I built:

  • A login & register page (login.html) with a glassmorphism card — a frosted glass effect using backdrop-filter: blur() over an anime background
